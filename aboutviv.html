Vivian Richards

The power player...


Sir Vivian Richards. You knew when he was coming. He gave "swagger" a whole new meaning. He was the most destructive batsman of his era, and while there are others with brilliant records, none could match his ability to rip apart bowling attacks like they were nothing.

Viv Richards is the original power player. The outgoing batsman was the fan fovrite. While he walked to the crease, sauntering, swaggering, arms windmilling slowly, the fans screamed and cheered him on. He would arrive at the crease, tap the pitch, and look in the bowlers eye. It was calculated menace and magnificent theatre from arguably the most devastating batsman of all time.



How were you to bowl against such a batsman? Well, you'd probably want to get him to the other end. Viv Richards' strengths were on the front foot. His strides were massive, eliminating the chance of an LBW, and creating a leg stump line of his own, where he could whack bowlers effortlessly through the midwicket region.

His power, however, was the main thing that defined is batting. His pulls and hooks were devestating, rocking back from his front-foor base to dispatch the ball from his eyeline in front of square. Another defining characteristic is that he did not wear a helmet. With the aura that he brought to the game, Viv Richards has left his mark on the history books.
