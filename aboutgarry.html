Sir Garfield Sobers

The most well rounded player...


Garry Sobbers was a genius. There's no denying it. he excelled at all aspects of the game. While Bradman's status as the greatest batsmen is under threat, no one asks any questions when Garry Sobers is called the greatest all rounder. He could bowl a perfect line and length with his fast-medium style, and could make runs almost anywhere.

His exceptional test batting average does not do justice to the manner in which he made the runs. Along with his memorable off-side play, his trademark powerful yet elegant style is what defines his batting. His mammoth score of 365* against Pakistan was truly an outstanding innings. He has multiple records, including hitting six consecutive sixes off an over from Malcolm Nash, when playing for the Rest of the World XI against Australia. This earned him the praise of Don Bradman, and many others as well.



Garry Sobers, though his batting was outstanding, could've been picked for the West Indian side as a bowler. He was remarkably versatile with the ball, bowling two types of spin, left-arm orthodox and wrist spin, but he was most notable for being an excellent opening medium-fast bowler. Without a doubt, Garry Sobers deserves recognition in this list.
