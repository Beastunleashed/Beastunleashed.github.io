Don Bradman

The Don

The man... The myth... The legend...


Arguably the greatest batsman of the game, the greatest cricketer of all time, and one of the most dominant sportsmen of all time, Don Bradman was the ultimate competitor. Only WG Grace matched his status remotely at that time. The Don's popularity, not only as a cricketer, but also as a selector and sage, has increased, as he has lived on into the 21st century.

Throughout the early 20th century, Don Bradman dominated the cricket scene. His ability to dispatch any bowler made him a great batsman. Only Jack Hobbs came close to his status as a player. Under him, the Australian team only lost the Ashes once, when the bewildered English side came up with the brutal system of bowling. Bodyline.



He greeted the crease 80 times in Tests, and tallied up 29 centuries. In his last Test innings, he required four runs to obtain an average of 100, bad unfortunately was out for 0. However, his average of 99.4 shows at what calibre he was able to play. Even though his batting was not the most technicaly correct, it was brilliant to watch, as he bombarded bowlers.
